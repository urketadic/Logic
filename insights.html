Comfort

When you have comfort as a core value you tend to settle with what you have, after achieving the point where you are satisfied with results you settle and start procrastinating instead of growing and keep improving. You also probably spend a lot of time during a day watching tv or searching entertainment on the internet.

Validation

Pinging to others for validation instead of pinging to yourself and reality.

Relationships

When you have Relationship as a core value you probably care only about your partner and the fear of being alone. Your actions were mostly directed towards finding a partner or maintain relationship.

Family

When you have Family as a core value you probably really attached to your family and do everything to please your family members even if it goes against yourself.

Logic

When you have Logic as your core value you don't let anything else guide you but reality.

Safety

When you have Safety as a core value you probably act only to provide safety, it can be financial or emotional safety

Happiness

Doing whatever makes you happy.

God

When you have god as your core value then probably all you do is dictated by your religion and you are ready to make sacrifices for the God you are believe in.

Core Values

Core value is concept that is connected to reward center in your brain, concept that you value the most over other values. Ego

Ego is defence mechanism in our brain that kick in when we feel like our beliefs are threatened. when active it make you unable to see information objectively as you try to defend your beliefs even if they are confronted with reality. Ego can be channelled through identity and they can strengthen each other. In order to dissolve ego you need to be aware of this mechanic in the first place and when it happens remember to not follow up on it and try to think unbiased about information that caused ego reaction. Sources: http://www.psychpage.com/learning/defenses.html

Attachements

Attachments are products of Identity, it's things that people identify with and don’t want to change them even when the attachment is damaging their life. Example: being attached to your family member will result in huge emotional crisis when something bad happen to that person. Detaching from something doesn’t mean that we stop feeling any emotion toward this thing, we will still feel the same but we are able to let it go and change.

Dogmas

Because of Probabilistic nature of universe, there is no such a thing as absolute truths. we can't be completely sure of anything. Dogmas and absolute truths are product of binary thinking. Truth always depends on the framework and context.

Labeling

Labeling is the mechanism where you label people and things as “good” bad” “useless” etc. It's product of binary thinking and it reduces your ability to see things for what they are as you generalize and don’t put things in the right context. Sources:
The Social Psychology of Stereotypes, by Banaji, Mahzarin R.
Toward an Ecological Theory of Social Perception, by McArthur, Leslie; Baron, Reuben
First impressions, by Willis, J.; Todorov, A.

Freedom

Freedom is self-contradictory concept that most of the people use as an excuse to justify their inaction. In reality your freedom can’t goes at the cost of other people freedom. Also there is no freedom without responsibility. Example: theoretically mother is free and can decide to don't care about her child and to let it die but she is also responsible for that child. Studies show that we even subconsciously make the decision even before we are aware of it, so there is no such a thing as true freedom. Once you understand it you will start doing what is right rather than shield yourself with this dysfunctional belief.

Responsibility

The extent to which you are responsible for something depends on your knowledge, when were kids we didn't know what consequences our actions have but as we grow up we start to be aware of that and that's when we are responsible for all the consequences of our inaction towards making the world a better place.

Selflessness

Selflessness is a state where our every action is for the benefit of others. it's the opposite of being selfish. you can become selfless by detaching from chasing good experience in order to do so you need to go through identity death. Identity death Is letting go of your identity, understanding that it’s just a mental construct that create lot of dissonance in your life, detaching from all the things that you valued and you become truly selfless.

Choiceless Awareness

Choiceless awareness is the knowledge that in reality we don’t have the choice, we always will chose most logical option in order to be consistent. From this awareness actions that are most impactful flow naturally. In order to develop choiceless awareness you need to understand insights on this site, understand our purpose, and then try to be consistent with it. You also need to know that freedom is self contradictory concept because it cannot exist without responsibility. Once you understand that it will become part of your awareness.

Identity

Identity is a mental construct that most people build while growing up in order to cope with intersubjectivity that is hyper individualistic. Identity is all the emotional attachments and labels that people have, it's a flod self image. It's build on past experiences and it inhibits our growth because it goes against our purpose of evolving. If you say or think this is mine, this is how I am, then most probably you have identity. Identity can cause a lot of dissonance and it has a lot of holes that's why even when people see that it's disfuncional they keep adding more layers and rationalizations in order to keep their identity.

Reality

Logic

Logic a consistent patterns that bring about reality. It’s the reason why we evolved from stardust to what we are now and thanks to logic itself we can better understand reality and ourselves.

Subjective

It's the reality that we experience. It may differ from the objective one because of dysfunctional beliefs or just limitations of our perception.

Experience

Experience Is everything that we feel and are aware of, its emerging property of the brain, because of limitation of our experience we can’t “see” objective reality, within experience everything is subjective. When we look at history of humankind we can clearly notice big growth and progress with cognitive revolution, that's because our experience as a feedback loop is such a powerful tool that allows us to change and evolve much quicker. Unfortunately nowadays we see that people wrongly see experience as a goal not as a tool, and by that they behave selfish chasing good experience which is complete misuse of the experience and going against evolution.

Feedback Loop

Feedback loop text.

Circle of Influence/Concern

The circle of concern contains things that we don’t have control over, like asking ourselves “was 9/11 an inside job?”. The circle of influence contains things that we have under control (saving lives, changing your lifestyle, spreading awareness). We should focus on the circle of influence, to start thinking in solutions rather than problems. If we are not thinking in circle of influence, we are part of the problem ourselves.
